Licenses ðŸ”—

There are two generally accepted Open Hardware licenses:

  • CERN Open Hardware Licence

  • TAPR Open Hardware License

Each can come with its own strategy. For example, the 2.0 version of the CERN open hardware license includes three versions:

  • "Weakly" reciprocal

  • "Strongly" reciprocal

  • Non-reciprocal

A reciprocal license is similar to copyleft for software licenses, meaning others who make use of your work must share their derivatives under the same license. This way, no one can take what you made, make changes, and turn it into a closed-source product.
